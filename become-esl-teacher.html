What Does an Online ESL Teacher Do?

An online ESL (English as a Second Language) teacher for Chinese students teaches English to children and sometimes adults in China through live video classes. Unlike traditional classroom teaching, everything happens through a computer — you teach from home, the student joins from China, and a virtual classroom platform connects you both in real time.

This has become one of the most accessible online careers for Filipinos because English fluency, not a teaching degree, is the primary requirement most companies look for. That said, certain technical and certification requirements still apply, which we'll cover next.

What Is Classin?

Classin is a virtual classroom platform — not a company — used by many Chinese ESL companies to deliver live lessons. Think of it as the "venue" where your class happens, similar to how Zoom or Google Meet work, but built specifically for teaching young English learners.

Classin includes built-in teaching tools most generic video call apps don't have:

  • An interactive whiteboard for writing and drawing during lessons
  • Reward stickers and gamified elements to keep young students engaged
  • Screen sharing for slides, videos, and lesson materials
  • Built-in audio and video specifically optimized for teaching

If you've never heard of Classin before reading this, that's completely normal — most aspiring ESL teachers discover Classin only after they start researching specific companies to apply to. It is the technical foundation you'll need to learn regardless of which Classin-based company hires you.

What Is a Demo Class — And Why It's the Hardest Part

A demo class is a short mock lesson, usually 5–10 minutes, that you perform during the hiring process. The company evaluates your lesson structure, confidence, pacing, and how well you use the platform's tools — all in real time.

This is where most candidates fail — not because they can't speak English well, but because they don't know what recruiters are specifically scoring them on. Common reasons candidates fail their demo:

  • Unfamiliarity with Classin's tools causes awkward pauses or technical fumbling
  • No clear lesson structure — jumping around without a beginning, middle, and end
  • Low energy or nervousness that doesn't translate well to young learners
  • Poor lighting, background, or camera setup that looks unprofessional

The good news — every one of these is fixable with practice and the right feedback before your actual demo, not after you've already been rejected.

How to Apply — Step by Step

  1. Prepare your requirements — device, internet, TEFL certificate, and video introduction
  2. Research and apply to Classin-based ESL companies such as Fancytalk or similar platforms
  3. Install and familiarize yourself with the Classin app before your scheduled demo
  4. Prepare and practice a structured demo lesson — ideally with feedback from someone who's done it before
  5. Complete your demo class evaluation with the hiring company
  6. Once approved, complete onboarding paperwork and begin teaching

How Much Can You Earn?

ESL teachers working with Chinese student platforms in the Philippines typically earn between ₱15,000 to ₱40,000 per month, depending on the company's rate structure, how many classes you teach, and your experience level over time. Most companies pay per class or per hour, meaning your income scales directly with the hours you're willing to teach.

This makes it an attractive option for fresh graduates building their first income stream, and for stay-at-home parents who need a flexible schedule around family responsibilities.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I become an online ESL teacher for Chinese students in the Philippines?
To become an online ESL teacher for Chinese students in the Philippines, you typically need a laptop or desktop with at least an Intel Core i3 processor, stable internet of at least 10 Mbps upload and download, a TEFL or TESOL certificate, and a professional video introduction. Most companies use platforms like Classin to deliver live online classes. After meeting these requirements, you apply to ESL companies, complete a demo class evaluation, and once hired, begin teaching scheduled classes.
What is Classin and why do ESL companies use it?
Classin is a virtual classroom platform widely used by Chinese ESL companies such as Fancytalk, HAIDA, and similar platforms to deliver live one-on-one and group English lessons. It includes built-in teaching tools like a whiteboard, reward stickers, screen sharing, and student progress tracking, designed specifically for teaching young Chinese learners online.
What is a demo class and why does it matter for getting hired?
A demo class is a short mock lesson that ESL companies require during the hiring process to evaluate a candidate's teaching ability, lesson structure, confidence, and platform skills before offering an actual teaching contract. Passing the demo class is the single most important step in getting hired as an ESL teacher for Chinese students, since most rejections happen at this stage rather than during the application itself.
Do I need teaching experience to become an ESL teacher for Chinese students?
No prior teaching experience is required to become an ESL teacher for Chinese students. Many companies hire fresh graduates, career shifters, and stay-at-home parents as long as they meet the technical requirements, hold or are willing to obtain a TEFL certificate, and can demonstrate basic teaching ability during the demo class evaluation.
How much can I earn teaching Chinese students online in the Philippines?
ESL teachers working with Chinese student platforms in the Philippines typically earn between ₱15,000 to ₱40,000 per month depending on the company, number of classes taught, and experience level. Income is usually based on a per-class or per-hour rate, allowing teachers to scale their earnings by taking on more sessions.
How long does it take to get hired as an online ESL teacher?
With proper preparation, most candidates can get hired as an online ESL teacher for Chinese students within 1 to 2 weeks. The timeline includes submitting an application, completing platform-specific training, passing a demo class evaluation, and finalizing onboarding paperwork with the hiring company.
